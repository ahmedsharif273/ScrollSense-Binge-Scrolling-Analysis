Building the measures

Two composite indices were constructed from the 24 Likert-scale items (1–5): a Binge-Scrolling Index (12 items covering compulsive use and emotional aftermath) and an Academic Impact Index (4 items on procrastination, study efficiency, focus, and anxiety). Reliability was checked before treating either as a single score.

Reading the findings

Nearly half of surveyed students fall into the "at-risk" persona, where compulsive scrolling tracks closely with procrastination and lost study focus. The strength of the correlation (r = .78) suggests binge-scrolling isn't just correlated with academic strain incidentally — it's one of the more consistent behavioral predictors in this sample. Academic year matters more than age or field of study, hinting the pattern builds over the course of a degree rather than being fixed by discipline.

Method notes

· n = 150, self-report survey
· 5-point Likert scale, 24 items
· Composite indices: item-mean
· K-means, k=3, standardized inputs
· ANOVA / t-tests for group comparisons
